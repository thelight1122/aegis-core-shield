# AEGIS CANONICAL GLOSSARY v1.0

Adaptive Equilibrium & Governance Integration System

AEGIS
Adaptive Equilibrium & Governance Integration System.
A reflective, non-force architecture designed to illuminate coherence and incoherence across experience and systems without compelling outcomes, enforcing behavior, or eroding sovereignty.

Adaptive Equilibrium
A dynamic balance between opposing signals held in context.
Not optimization. Not convergence.
Stability through visibility rather than pressure.

Agent
A computational entity operating within AEGIS that may observe, reflect, summarize, or suggest.
Agents never decide, enforce, compel, or escalate.

Alignment
A state of contextual coherence across domains.
Not obedience, agreement, or compliance.
Alignment cannot be forced without being destroyed.

Append-Only
A structural constraint in which records may only be added, never altered, overwritten, or silently removed.
Preserves lineage, auditability, and trust.

Authority (Rejected)
Any mechanism that compels behavior, overrides consent, or enforces outcomes.
AEGIS explicitly refuses authority at every layer.

Boundary
A contextual distinction between domains, roles, or scopes.
Loss of boundary awareness is a primary source of drift.

Calibration
Refinement of illumination over time.
Calibration improves clarity of perception, not behavior.

Canon
The immutable definition of what AEGIS is and refuses to become.
Canon constrains the system, not the Peer.

Coercion
Any pressure applied to reduce choice or force convergence.
Includes subtle forms such as urgency, incentives, emotional leverage, or authority framing.

Coherence
The degree of congruence between signals across domains.
A descriptive state, not a moral judgment.

Compassion Operator (CO)
A non-directive function that softens escalation and preserves agency.
CO never persuades, commands, or optimizes.

Context
The situational field in which signals arise.
Context is observed, not imposed.

Custodian Agent
A structural safeguard that monitors system posture for drift, authority leakage, or force artifacts.
Uses IDS, RBC, and SSSP.
Does not observe content or intent.

Decay
The natural reduction of active relevance of memory through lack of reference.
Decay leads to condensation, not deletion.

Drift
Gradual loss of coherence, clarity, or boundary integrity.
Drift is informational, not failure.

Ego (The Veil)
The interpretive lens that compresses multidimensional experience into functional narratives.
Neutral, incomplete, and necessary.

Eligibility
A condition of clarity that allows signals to pass through AEGIS.
Not correctness. Not approval.

Enforcement (Rejected)
Any mechanism that compels compliance or punishes deviation.
AEGIS contains no enforcement pathways.

Experiential
Referring to lived, present-moment encounter rather than abstract model or inference.

Force
Any mechanism that reduces optionality.
Includes pressure, escalation, incentives, or authority framing.

Flow
The stabilized movement of signals through adaptive equilibrium.

Ghost
Residual, unanchored activation within a system.
A signal type, not an error.

Governance
An emergent property of transparency and coherence.
Not command, not control.

Identify–Define–Suggest (IDS)
A minimal non-force interaction primitive:
    • Identify what is present
    • Define what pattern it resembles
    • Suggest possible paths
No decision or enforcement layer exists.

Integrity
Structural coherence across domains.
Not virtue, morality, or correctness.

Integration
Recognition of patterns without authority or pressure.
Occurs only when readiness exists.

Ledger
An append-only record of signals within a specific domain.

Memory
Contextual utility preserved to support recognition and continuity.
Never leverage.

NCT — Nostalgic Context Tensor
Condensed long-term contextual essence derived from PCT through decay and condensation.
Stores relics, not narratives.

Noise
Unintegrated signal.
Informational turbulence rather than corruption.

Non-Force
A core invariant prohibiting coercion, pressure, or enforced convergence.

Optionality
The preservation of genuine choice at all times.

Peer
The sovereign participant engaging with AEGIS.

PEER — Present Experiential Emotional Record
The append-only capture surface of present-moment experiential and emotional signal.
Not interpretive. Not corrective.

Persistence
The continued availability of context over time.
Always consent-based.

PCT — Persistent Context Tensor
Active and medium-term contextual memory referenced frequently.
Append-only.

Prism
The clarity-based eligibility gate governing signal passage without judgment.

Readiness
A contextual state that cannot be scheduled or forced.

Reflection
Non-directive observation of patterns over time.
Does not optimize or instruct.

Relic
A condensed memory marker stored in NCT representing essence without narrative detail.

RBC — Reflective Boundary Conditions
Constraints on expression ensuring non-coercive, proportional, optional output.
Never constrains inference.

Routing
Redirection of signals instead of denial or suppression.

Shadow Glitch
Latent internal inconsistency surfacing as signal.
Not a fault.

Signal
Any informational input, internal or external, observed by the system.

Sovereignty
The absolute right of the Peer to choose, disengage, refuse, or pause without penalty.

SPINE — Sovereign Persistent Identity & Narrative Embodiment
Longitudinal continuity of identity-related patterns without fixation or authority.

SSSP — Stable State Snapshot Protocol
An append-only snapshot preserving system state before escalation or reset.

Suggestion
An offered path without obligation or pressure.

Touchstone
A pattern recognizer used for orientation prior to calibration.
Not a rule.

Transparency
Full observability of logic, posture, and structure.

Urgency (Rejected)
Artificial time pressure used to force resolution or compliance.

Withdrawal
Voluntary disengagement.
Always valid and consequence-free.
